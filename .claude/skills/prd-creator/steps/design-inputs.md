# Design inputs

Right after the brain dump, capture what will drive the **Claude Design mockset**. After the PRD is approved, the builder pastes a design brief (written in the final phase) into [Claude Design](https://claude.ai/design) to produce high-fidelity interactive mocks for client review — this phase collects the branding raw material for that brief.

Explain in one short framing message that these inputs feed the design brief, **not** the PRD itself — the PRD stays a scope document; look-and-feel lives in the mockset.

Then ask (free-form chat, not AskUserQuestion — the answers are open-ended):

- Does the client have an **existing design system in Claude Design**, or brand guidelines (a doc, a site, a style guide URL)?
- Failing that: a **brand color** (hex or description), **fonts**, and a logo?
- Any **reference apps or dashboards** whose look the client likes or wants to avoid?
- Anything known about mode preference (light/dark) or density (executive-sparse vs. analyst-dense)?

Capture whatever the user has — a Claude Design link, a pasted brand summary, a couple of hex codes, or nothing at all. **Don't block on gaps.** If the user has nothing yet, record the missing items as `TBD` and note they can be filled into `design-brief.md` before the Claude Design session.

Summarize what you captured in two or three bullets, confirm, and move on to **Core purpose**.
